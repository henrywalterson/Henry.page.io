VIETCHARM

Redesigning a cultural dining show's website to match the premium tone and refine the conversion-ready experience, starting from mobile.

UX/UI REDESIGN

Role

UX/UI Designer (Solo)

Type

Self-initiated Redesign

Year

2025

Scope

Homepage · Booking flow · Desktop & Mobile

PROBLEM

The homepage made it hard for users to find their way and make confident decisions.

Three different nav items all lead to the same page. The feature section overwhelms users with text. The ticket section gives no spatial context to justify the price difference.

OUTCOME →

Clearer navigation and visual decision support reduce hesitation — keeping potential buyers engaged long enough to convert.

PROBLEM

Booking flow has unnecessary friction at the point of highest purchase intent.

Brand content appears before the booking form. There's no persistent order summary, no progress indicator, and no refund policy near the CTA.

OUTCOME →

Fewer steps, persistent order visibility, and trust signals at checkout lower abandonment at the highest-intent moment in the funnel.

PROCESS

Without access to analytics or heatmaps, I audited the site the way a real user would, then applied structured heuristic evaluation.

Nielsen's Usability Heuristics
Baymard Institute checkout flow research
Luxury hospitality brand reference

Evidence: Lighthouse score · WAVE accessibility report · date-picker bug

WHAT COULD BE IMPROVED

Home page

  • 3 nav items lead to the same page
  • No fallback when video fails to load
  • Feature section overwhelms with text
  • Ticket section lacks spatial context
  • Schedule is long and hard to scan

WHAT COULD BE IMPROVED

Booking page

  • Content appears before the booking form
  • No step indicator mid-flow
  • No persistent order summary
  • Redundant "Book now" CTA on nav bar when in booking flow

WHAT I HAVE DONE

Homepage

  • 3 nav items lead to the same page → Consolidated into 2 clear CTAs in hero
  • No fallback when video fails to load → Added image fallback
  • Feature section overwhelms with text → Replaced with expanding panels
  • Ticket section lacks spatial context → Added interactive seating map
  • Schedule is long and hard to scan → Condensed into 3 main sections

WHAT I HAVE DONE

Booking Page

  • Content appears before the booking form → Form moved to the top, content removed
  • No step indicator mid-flow → Added 2-step progress bar
  • No persistent order summary → Sticky sidebar with itemized breakdown
  • Redundant "Book now" CTA → Removed from nav when in booking flow

Lesson learned

  • User intent dictates content hierarchy.
  • Simplify first, scale complexity later.
  • Responsive means adapting behavior, not resizing.
  • Decisions were validated through heuristic evaluation under data constraints.
  • Trust signals matter at the point of conversion.
  • Structural friction kills conversion before visuals even matter.

What's next

If I had more time, I'd validate these decisions with real users, particularly first-time visitors arriving from social ads, to confirm whether the changes actually reduce friction or surface new ones I haven't seen yet.
